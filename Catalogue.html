Food Catalogue

1. Hainanese Chicken Rice

Hainanese chicken rice is a dish of poached chicken and seasoned rice, served with chilli sauce and usually with cucumber garnishes.The quality of chicken stock is crucial to this dish, and you can tell by the steamed rice oozing with flavour and a fragrant aroma. Pour some dipping sauce over the chicken and give it a go.

Recommended place to eat: Tian Tian Hainanese Chicken Rice

  • Address: 1 Kadayanallur Street, #01-10/11 Maxwell Food Centre, Singapore 069184
  • Operating Hours: 10AM-5:30PM, daily
  • Closest MRT Station: Chinatown

2. Chilli Crab

You can't visit Singapore without rolling up your sleeves and diving into a plate of iconic chili crab. Chilli crab is a seafood dish in which stir-fried crab is coated with sweet, savoury, and spicy tomato-based sauce. This dish is often accompanied by steam or deep-fried buns (also known as mantous).

Recommended place to eat: Don Signature Crab

  • Address: Block 206, Toa Payoh North 1, #01-1197, Singapore 310206
  • Operating Hours: 12NN-8:30PM, daily
  • Closest MRT Station: Braddell

3. Bak Gut Teh

Bak kut teh is the ultimate comfort food in Singapore, with hearty broth sporting thick cuts of meat, especially comforting in rainy weather. This dish literally translates to "pork bone tea", but you won't find any tea in this pork-laden soup. In fact, tea is usually enjoyed alongside bak kut teh, to cut through the heaviness of the meat.

Recommended place to eat: Ng Ah Sio

  • Address: Ng Ah Sio has four branches in Singapore.
  • Operating Hours: Varies per branch

4. Char Kway Teow

Char kway teow is made of a mix of flat rice noodles and yellow wheat noodles, stir-fried over high heat with light and dark soy sauce, garlic, chilli, lup cheong (Chinese waxed sausage), egg, fishcake and beansprouts. The dish is usually served on a banana leaf to enhance the scent, with a wedge of lime squeezed over the dish. A dash of sour-spicy chilli sauce is added on the side for an extra kick.

Recommended place to eat: Cockle Fried Kway Teow

  • Address: 75 Lorong 5 Toa Payoh, #01-08, Singapore 310075
  • Operating Hours: Wed-Sun 5pm to 9pm
  • Closest MRT Station: Toa Payoh

5. Bak Chor Mee

Bak Chor Mee, also known as “minced meat noodles” in either in the Hokkien or Teochew dialect, is a staple dish in Singapore's street food. It's a bowl of noodles (usually dry) and filled with minced pork, pork balls and livers, stewed mushrooms with a strong black vinegar flavour.

Recommended place to eat: Hill Street Tai Hwa Pork Noodle

  • Address: 66 Crawford Lane, #01-12, Singapore 190466
  • Operating Hours: 9:30AM-9PM, Tue-Sun (closed Mondays)
  • Closest MRT Station: Lavender

6. Mala Xiang Guo

The dish consists of a variety of picked ingredients (such as sliced meats, spam, mushroom, vegetables to instant noodles) wok-fried in high heat together with tongue-numbing sauce. Customers can usually pick from ”Xiao La” (little spicy), ”Zhong La” (medium spicy), to ”Da La” (very spicy).

Recommended place to eat: Ri Ri Hong

  • Address: 32 New Market Road, People's Park Complex Food Centre, #01-1036 and #01-1152, Singapore 050032
  • Operating Hours: 10:00am - 09:30pm daily
  • Closest MRT Station: Chinatown

7. Hokkien Mee

The dish typically consist of yellow noodles and rice vermicelli stir-fried with slices of prawn, squid, pork belly, egg and pork lard. It is often served with sambal sauce and lime for that added citrusy flavour. The plate of noodles comes with a sizeable amount of toppings including prawns and pieces of pork belly.

Recommended place to eat: Come Daily Fried Hokkien Prawn Mee

  • Address: #02-27, Blk 127, Toa Payoh Lor 1, S310127
  • Operating Hours: Tuesday to Sunday, 09:30 - 18:00
  • Closest MRT Station: Toa Payoh

8. Ban Mian

Ban Mian is considered a fairly simple dish, of handmade noodles (usually rolled over a pasta maker), cooked in soup typically, and added with ingredients such as minced pork, anchovies, mushrooms and spinach. The base of the entire meal is the soup, so there are numerous variations in ingredients, stocks and noodle shapes. In many instances, the completed soup is topped with an egg that is cooked in the hot liquid above the noodles.

Recommended place to eat: Madam Leong Ban Mian

  • Address: 7 Maxwell Road, #02-109, Amoy Street Food Centre, Singapore 069111
  • Operating Hours: Mon, Wed-Sat 10am to 2pm, Tue 10:30am to 2pm
  • Closest MRT Station: Tanjong Pagar

9. Nasi Lemak

Nasi lemak, the Malay fragrant rice dish cooked in creamy coconut milk and pandan leaf, is one of Singaporean's favourite dishes. Add some crispy chicken wings, deep-fried fish, otah otah, fried ikan bilis and peanuts, eggs and cucumber slices for a more indulgent treat. Not forgetting the much-need sambal chilli sauce for that oomph.

Recommended place to eat: Bali Nasi Lemak

  • Address: 2 Lor 15 Geylang Road, Singapore 388596
  • Operating Hours: 5:30PM-4AM, Mon-Sat (closed on Sundays)
  • Closest MRT Station: Aljunied

10. Ice-cream Sandwich

It's a brick of ice cream and wrapped up in a slice of bread. There are many different flavours of ice cream to choose from i.e. strawberry, peppermints, chocolate chips, vanilla, mocha and durian. Some vendors will wrap it up with a slice of white bread or rainbow coloured bread (lightly sweetened with pandan flavour) and some may offer wafers.

Recommended place to eat: Singaporean Ice Cream Sandwich

  • Address: Orchard Road (along the shopping mall street from Ion Orchard to Takashimaya)
  • Operating Hours: No fixed time but usually can be spotted in the afternoon.
  • Closest MRT Station: Orchard
